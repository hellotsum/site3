Story


Since the 16th century, beignets have been enjoyed during Mardi Gras by the French. The term “beignet” itself means “fritter” in French, much like a doughnut or a piece of fried dough.


Beignets made their debut in New Orleans during the 1700s, thanks to French-Creole colonists who settled in the region.
